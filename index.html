What is the sunshine list?

The Public Sector Salary Disclosure Act, 1996 makes Ontarioâ€™s public sector more open and accountable to taxpayers. The act requires organizations that receive public funding from the Province of Ontario to make public, by March 31 each year, the names, positions, salaries and total taxable benefits of employees paid $100,000 or more in the previous calendar year.

The trend on the sunshine list this past decade?

  • The number of recipients has been steadily growing
  • The total amount paid to the recipients has been steadily growing
  • The judiciary branch of the government has been consistently the highest paid sector
  • The majority of those earning 500K/y are from public health and power generation sectors
